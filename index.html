Problems and Challenges today’s Media

For the decades we have known that the current media system is mainly controlled by a
few interest groups, they determine what is a story and what isn’t.

The problems and challenges of today’s media

At any given moment an untold number of good, bad, horrible, funny and or uplifting stories are developing all over the world.

In our view there are 3 types of stories, BREAKING, DEVELOPING and OLD.

When, How and If those stories get to you will be determined by a lot of factors some of which are listed below:

  • The medium or network by which it’s sourced before it gets to you.
  • The groups of interests it may impact along the way, this impact can be financial as well as political.
  • The region or country where you live, as local authorities my seek to keep the incident localized.
  • Local Networks capacity to get someone onto the scene on time, even those with news choppers fail to get there at the moment it breaks.
  • Editors willingness to allow a story to move forward without succumbing to pressure from above.
  • The networks ability to determine the veracity or authenticity of the story and its actors as well as the setting of it.
  • If its printed then its old, edited and largely manipulated.
  • With the current system, by design and how it is structued you will always have an older version of the story that is handpicked and curated for you.

Too Slow to get to there on time.

Even networks with massive resources in some of the largest metro areas simply cannot get there on time. By the time they manage to get a manicured face onto the scene with a broadcast team it is no longe a breaking story, it is rather a developing one and they are actively trying to collect details on what transpired before.

If the incident is across borders the network is leftt scouring social media and other outlets to be able to bring it to their audiences homes.

Sourced and curated for a desired effect

Most news and stories you see, hear or read about are sourced from a massive central source that puts forward a narrative that then sets the tone for any incident.

By the time you see it, your reactions have already been expected and planned for. You may not even be getting news but actually a scripted version of events if you get the events at all and in the correct sequence.

Fake news...

In an attempt to keep up with social media, many networks have moved to retweet or re-broadcast content they pick up online. Sometimes they run with a story or narrative that is entirely false and leads to embarrassing retractions.

As technology grows, with deepfakes among other advances, this will only increase and make it even harder for journalists to keep up with the onslaught of unverified content made available to the public at large.

Our Solution

There are 5.27 billion unique mobile phone users in the world today, according to the latest
data from GSMA Intelligence.

Every phone is a mic and camera

With RedEyed every mobile device is a potential microphone and camera, turning every user into a potential correspondent for the breaking story.

This completely decentralizes the source of the story and eliminates any narrative beyond what is captured by those in the immediate vicinity.

Sourced and curated by the community

With RedEyed the source of every story is a community user, it is confirmed by other community users with live video from the same scene and it is unedited, unscripted and live.

The way our app works, nothing can be uploaded into our network unless it is recorded live. Every piece of footage is unique and every user own’s each piece of footage until ownership is transfered via auction in our NFT marketplace.

Instant access to the news room

Our NFT marketplace allows News organizations to BID on Breaking stories by the minute as they go live.

This gives the News room an opportunity to keep thier audiences informed until they can have a crew on the scene to transition into a developing story. Once ownership is transfered, the new owner gets direct access and can use it at their discretion. RedEyed always retains broadcast access while the story is live and as proof of origin. If it was on RedEyed then it was here first. If you hold the token its yours forever.

The Solution was already here.

On a daily basis the large majority of all mobile devices are active on the minute of every hour.

Each of these devices has a camera with few exceptions.

What happens if we turn each lens into a potential correspondent for media outlets and each user into a digital reporter? :

  • Mobile users are always on the scene at the exact moment a story breaks.
  • With our App each user can only shoot live, unedited, uncut footage.
  • The footage is streamed live into our platform where it is available to our entire Apps audience.
  • The same footage is also immediately made available to all Media Outlets who may wish to expand it to their local audience as it happens live.
  • Every broadcast is a unique NFT assigned to the user on the scene, so the source is unequivocal.
  • News Organizations will be able to Bid instantly for every piece of live footage as it enters our marketplace and buy the rights to re-transmit to their respectiveaudiences. All profits excepting Minting and platfrom fees go the creator.
  • A story is validated as more POVs are streamed for the same incident or validated by the community or as it is developed by local media.
  • Every piece of footage is documented from its creation for the rest of its existence, a chain of custody supported by its unique address.
